Making a sale or collecting money for service

1.Click on add products button to add your products or services and their prices. 2.Add your employee details using the add employee button.
3.Click on sale or payment button and select the services or products your are selling.
4.Select mode of sale or payment options are cash,bank or mpesa
5.Enter the total price charged in the respective field.
6.Enter customer payment in the respective field though its optional depending on your business
7.Click the submit button and you will be redirected to the receipt
8.There is an option of printing or sharing receipt ,choose either.
9.For credit sale ,click on sell on credit and the follow the same steps.

Making a purchase

1.Click on the purchase button.
2.Enter the products you are purchasing in the respective field.
3.Enter the total price charged for the products.
4.Choose your mode of payment.
5.Select employee making the purchase
6.Then click on submit purchase button and you will taken to the purchases page .
7.To purchase on credit click on purchase on credit and follow the same steps.

Adding an expense

1.Click on the expense button.
2.Choose the expense option from the provided options
3.Enter the total expense.
4.select employee making the transaction.
6.Then click on submit

Reseting/balancing accounts

1.Go to the home dashboard i.e accounts and the total amount
2.Check the outstanding amount in sales eg if its 8000 make a sale of -8000 and submit
3.The same applies for expense account eg if expenses is 3000 make an expense of -3000 and submit
4.The same applies for purchase accounts eg if purchases is 8000 make a purchase of -8000 and submit
5.The same applies for processed goods accounts eg if processed goods is 8000 make a transaction of -8000 and submit
outstanding balances in the dashboard should finally be zero.

Deleting transactions

1.Go to the home dashboard i.e accounts and the total amount
2.If you want to delete all sales,click on the sales total amount and delete continously until the figure is zero
3.The same applies for purchases and expenses accounts.
